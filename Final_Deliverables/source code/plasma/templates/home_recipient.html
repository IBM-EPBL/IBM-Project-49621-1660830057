When is a plasma transfusion needed?

People often need plasma transfusions in serious medical situations. Typically, plasma transfusions are used to stop or prevent bleeding due to blood clotting problems. A plasma transfusion can also help increase blood volume and prevent shock. A plasma transfusion may be needed for:

  • some types of open-heart surgery
  • transplant surgery
  • severe liver disease or failure
  • different types of clotting deficiencies
  • trauma or shock
  • severe burns
  • severe infections

What are the risks of a plasma infusion?

In general, plasma infusions are safe. There’s a low risk of complications. If side effects do occur, they might include:

  • soreness, bruising, or pain at the needle insertion site
  • infections
  • allergic reactions
  • reactions to the plasma
  • transfusion-associated circulatory overload
  • transfusion-related acute lung injury
